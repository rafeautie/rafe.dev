## Setup

1. Each player controls exactly **one** race-car. Pick your race-car colors.
2. Every game is a seven-race **Standard** championship (no alternate race types).
3. Create one unique suit of cards for **each** race-car (use placeholder icons such as ▲, ◆, ■, ● until the final symbols are chosen). Every suit contains the full card list described below.
4. Combine all suits into a single deck (cars × 13 cards) and confirm the total card count matches the number of cars in play.
5. Shuffle the combined deck thoroughly, then deal the cards face down to the cars in rotation until every car has the same number of cards.

## Card Ranks

| Card                | Value / Effect                                                                                         |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Extend (1)          | Move one space forward. (1 in a challenge)                                                             |
| Extend (2)          | Move one space forward. (2 in a challenge)                                                             |
| Drafting Extend (3) | Move one space forward. (3 in a challenge) <br>**Note**: The leader cannot play this card to extend.   |
| Standard (4–11)     | Face value in a challenge.                                                                             |
| Champion (12)       | 12 in a challenge (highest card).                                                                      |
| Redline card (R)    | 0 in a challenge. Can be played *with* any other card to **add 2** to that card, e.g. (12) + (R) = 14. |

## Playing the Game (Race Flow)

1. Place your race-car on top of the stack to show you are ready.
2. Each car selects a card (if you have a Redline, you can pick two) to qualify. Put the intended card face down on the top of your car’s stack.
3. Drivers reveal their cards simultaneously.
4. Highest card (or two with R) wins. In qualifying, ties are broken by highest die roll.
5. The car that played the **lowest** card is placed at the **end** of the board. Place the remaining cars in descending order by card rank (last to first). Played cards are discarded face-up.
6. Race starts with the last-place car challenging the car directly ahead.
	- Think of the race track as an infinite single-file lane. Moving forward takes the next open space in that lane. If the space is empty, a gap opens behind the mover; if it is occupied, the two cars swap positions.
7. Each car (on its turn) plays a card (or two, if using an R). Highest card(s) wins.
8. If the car behind wins, they **switch positions**.
9. Ties go to the car already ahead.
10. Play continues with the winning car challenging the next car directly ahead.
11. You must be directly behind another racecar to *challenge*. Once a car **loses** a challenge, its turn ends.
12. If a car passes **two** racecars on its turn, its turn ends.
13. If a racecar wins a challenge and has an **open space** in front, it **can** move one space forward using an Extend card. Only *one space*, no matter the number on the Extend.
14. The leader cannot use the **Drafting Extend (3)** card to extend.
15. When a car extends, its turn ends.
16. If you cannot extend, you **must discard** to end your turn.
17. After the leading car’s turn is done, the next lap starts with the **last-place** car.
18. The race ends when a racecar runs out of cards. Continue play with the next car ahead, finishing the final lap all the way to first place (even if some cars are out of cards).
19. Points are scored based on finishing position:

* 1st place → 9 points
* 2nd place → 6 points
* 3rd place → 4 points
* 4th place → 3 points
* 5th place → 2 points
* 6th place → 1 point

20. A **Championship** consists of **seven races**. Score for both teams and drivers.

## Notes on Play

* You must always play a card (whether to challenge, extend, or discard).
* You can pass and then extend, but you **cannot** extend and then pass in the same turn.
* Only **one** Redline card may be played at a time. If two Redlines are played accidentally, both are discarded for no value.
* A Redline card may be used to discard **two** cards.
* If a racecar runs out of cards before finishing its turn, play passes to the racecar ahead.
* The next lap will **not** start if any racecar has run out of cards.
* It’s important to keep track of how many cards each opponent has. When asked, players must **show** the number of cards they have left.
* Your car does **not** have to use an Extend card even if you have one — you may choose to discard instead.
